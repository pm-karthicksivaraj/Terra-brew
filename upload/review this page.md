# review this page

Terra Brew’s page is strong on positioning and feature breadth, but you can tighten messaging, structure, and differentiation to convert better.

## Positioning \& Message Clarity

- The core value proposition (“\#1 Coffee Traceability \& EUDR Compliance Software”) is clear but generic; it doesn’t show why it’s “\#1” or what’s uniquely different.[^1_1]
- You clearly target producers, cooperatives, exporters, importers, certification bodies, and labs, but all six get equal weight, which can dilute the message for the primary buyer persona (likely exporters/cooperatives).[^1_1]

Suggestions:

- Add a single **ROI**-driven promise under the hero, e.g. “Cut EUDR paperwork time by X% and unlock EU buyers in Y weeks.”
- Prioritize 1–2 primary personas in the hero (e.g. exporters \& cooperatives), then mention others lower on the page.


## Hero, CTA, and Above-the-Fold

- Hero headline and subheadline are strong, but the block of live prices plus long subtitle pushes your primary CTAs (“Start Free Trial / View Demo / Free EUDR Readiness Assessment”) down and makes the top visually busy.[^1_1]
- Having three CTAs side by side is powerful, but they compete with each other and don’t indicate which path is recommended.[^1_1]

Suggestions:

- Keep live prices but visually contain them (e.g. a slim ticker or side card) so the hero text and main CTA dominate.[^1_1]
- Make one **primary** CTA (e.g. “Book Live Demo”) and mark the others as secondary (“Start Free Trial”, “Free EUDR Assessment”), or move “Free EUDR Assessment” into a dedicated section.[^1_1]
- Consider adding a short proof line under the buttons (e.g. “Trusted by cooperatives in Vietnam, Brazil, Ethiopia, and Kenya”).[^1_1]


## Features \& Structure

- The feature section is comprehensive: traceability, EUDR, blockchain, analytics, deforestation, certifications, procurement, and RFQ/trading are all clearly explained.[^1_1]
- However, almost every block is similar length and depth, so the user gets a “wall of benefits” without clear hierarchy of what matters for whom.[^1_1]

Suggestions:

- Group features under 3–4 **outcomes** instead of 8 flat bullets, for example: “Win EU Market Access,” “Control Farm-to-Cup Traceability,” “Automate Certification \& Audits,” “Run Profitable Trade Operations.”[^1_1]
- For each persona section (producers, cooperatives, exporters, certification bodies) explicitly label 2–3 “Top benefits” rather than 6 equal bullets.[^1_1]
- Add at least one specific numeric example (even hypothetical/benchmarked) in a feature (“Automated due diligence cut document preparation time by 60% for exporters”).[^1_1]


## Technical \& Trust Signals

- Security and infrastructure are well articulated: AES-256, RBAC, tenant isolation, blockchain immutability, SOC 2, audit logging—this is unusually detailed for an LP and builds trust with serious buyers.[^1_1]
- The multi-entity architecture and global coverage (VN, BR, ET, KE) with languages and currencies are strong for differentiation versus generic “supply chain SaaS.”[^1_1]

Suggestions:

- Pull a short **Security summary** up near the hero or just above the fold (“SOC 2 infrastructure, AES-256 encryption, tenant isolation, and blockchain audit trail included by default”).[^1_1]
- For the global coverage section, add micro-proof to each market (e.g., “Metrang Coffee — Dak Lak”, “Cooxupé — 5,000+ farmers”) but also consider one logo strip or mini-case for each region.[^1_1]


## Conversion \& Content Depth

- FAQ content is excellent: EUDR explanation, blockchain value, supported certifications, small cooperative suitability, and deforestation risk are all answered in a buyer-centric way.[^1_1]
- CTAs are repeated at the end (“Get Started Now / View Live Demo / Free EUDR Assessment”), which is good, but there is no social proof block (case study snapshot, testimonial, or metrics) between features and FAQ.[^1_1]

Suggestions:

- Insert a **“Proven with X customers”** section just before FAQ: 2–3 short testimonials or mini case cards (“Cooxupé: automated RA certification across 5,000+ farmers”).[^1_1]
- Make the final CTA section more urgent and specific: “Get EUDR-ready before the next harvest cycle” rather than generic “Ready to digitize your coffee supply chain?”.[^1_1]
- Consider a micro-form CTA for “Free EUDR Readiness Assessment” (email + role + country) to capture leads from visitors not ready to start a trial.[^1_1]
